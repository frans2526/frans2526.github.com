Bonjour Montreal

Mar 23rd, 2013

Bonjour !
I’m in Montreal since one big month but I didn’t have the time to write some post. So the big good news is I got a job in Equation Humaine as a web developer. It’s an really amazing place with a lot of space and great people.
I found also a roommate in a nice place in Montreal, the people are friendly and it’s nice because most of the people who live in the building are also student or young professional.
We had a snow storm this week and so I saw one of the things that I wanted to see in Canada, a lot of snow :-D But that was the last time this season, I think because now the temparture goes more in the positif.

The last weekend with two of my co-workers we went to the office to watch the first Formula 1 GP. At 11PM to 4AM and that was a little bit crazy but that was a really good moment. We had six screens with the two on the left the stats, on the middle top the weather on the bottom the live and on the rigth somes twitter feeds.

About the city, I think is more an “European” city and I can buy a lot of EU brand that I couldn’t find in Van. The building are also more cachet than in Van and the city is smaller than Toronto so I prefere that. The public transport is really good, there are a lot of metro station every where and the price is cheaper than in Van 91$ vs 77$ in Montreal.

That’s it for today ;-)

Hello and welcome in the cold!
I’m in Toronto in my friend house, and outside it’s freezing, it’s -20C, that change from Vancouver with 6C…
But I like that and I’m really happy to be here. My friend is a hockey referee so we went to an arena. He had to work and I went with him to watch the three games, even if he said that wouldn’t be good games, one of them was good and the two others was OK.

After that, we went to his compatibility class, that wasn’t really interristing for me.
And when we had finished to work, we went to Waterloo, ON because it’s the city of RIM, no I’m just kiding it’s not for this reason, we went there to met one of his friend. That was really stunning because I was in Canada, speaking with two canadien english.

Goodbye Vancouver

Jan 18th, 2013

After less than four months in Vancouver, I have to say goodbye to all my new friends that I met during my trip in Van. It’s really difficult for me to do that because I met so many amazing people here but I want to move somewhere else.
Somewhere, where I can see some snow and not all this rain everyday… (I’m in Canada not in Belgium :-D)
So I decide to go first in Toronto to see a canadian friend but before I had to do my farewell party in the Pint :-p This name will be unforgettable for all my life.

After I want to go in Montreal or Quebec but I don’t know yet… I like to speak in english and the english culture but I think that will be easier for me to find a job in Quebec where I can speak french.

Unfortunately, I don’t keep only a good memorie of Van, this reason was my job in Wishpond, after only one month, I had already a bad impression of this place. My co-workers was funny and genial but the direction was totaly awfull and disrespectful.
We was at least between 40 and 45 persons in a small office, I had the habit to say to my colleague that we was in “Auschwitz”. Imagine to work in a subway, it’s the same but that was not the worst, they wanted always more “money” (for the Belgium people see the Luminis ads) and also I learn nothing except maybe “how you don’t have to manage a company” :-D
Just for remind you they didn’t pay us for the work that we did there…

Now it’s the past lets go Toronto!
